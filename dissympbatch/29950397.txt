  A 19-month-old girl with a history of asthma and atopic dermatitis<disease> presented to her pediatrician because of parental concerns of pallor and fatigue<symptom>. On dietary history , it was discovered that she was a picky eater and consumed 26 oz of homogenous milk daily. Her physical examination was unremarkable aside from pallor , and both her height and weight plotted between the 50th and 75th percentile for age. Therefore , she was investigated for iron deficiency anemia<symptom> and indeed her blood work was consistent. Despite appropriate iron supplementation and dietary milk restriction , there was no improvement in her hemoglobin or iron studies.
