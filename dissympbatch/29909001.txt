  Postural tachycardia<symptom> syndrome<disease> ( PoTS) is a multifactorial syndrome<disease> defined by an increase in heart rate ≥ 30 bpm , within 10minutes of standing ( or during a head up tilt test to at least 60 °) , in absence of orthostatic hypotension<symptom>. It is associated with symptoms of cerebral hypoperfusion that are worse when upright and improve in supine position. Patients have an intense fatigue<symptom> with a high incidence on quality of life. This syndrome<disease> can be explained by many pathophysiological mechanisms. It can be associated with Ehlers-Danlos disease and some autoimmune disorders. The treatment is based on nonpharmacological measures and treatment with propranolol , fludrocortisone or midodrine.
