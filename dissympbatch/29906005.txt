  Papular purpuric gloves and socks syndrome<disease> ( PPGSS) is a self-limited exanthem that presents as painful or pruritic edema<symptom> , erythema , petechiae<symptom> , and purpura of the palms and soles with occasional extension to the dorsal hands and feet. The majority of PPGSS cases reported in the literature are associated with parvovirus B19 and occur in children and young adults. In a recent literature search , there were 11 PPGSS cases in adults with none reporting Epstein-Barr virus ( EBV) as a viral etiology. However , there have been PPGSS cases related to EBV in children. We report the case of a 72-year-old man with PPGSS associated with Epstein-Barr virus.
