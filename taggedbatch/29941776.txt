 Cardiovascular disease ( CVD) is a major cause of death and disability , especially among people living in the rural , southern United States. Rural African Americans are often diagnosed with CVD earlier in life , and they bear a disproportionate burden of CVD risk factors , morbidity , and mortality. Health equity among historically underserved , rural populations can potentially be attained through culturally relevant interventions that teach people skills to stay well and avoid CVD-related risk and diagnoses. The purpose of this secondary analysis was to determine the effect of an evidence-based intervention on cardiovascular health knowledge and the stages of change toward the action and maintenance phases.
