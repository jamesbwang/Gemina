 Locally advanced breast cancer ( LABC) is associated with substantial risk of occult metastases. The patients with LABC have high rate of systemic relapse , suggesting inadequacy of the current conventional staging in detecting the occult metastatic spread. This prospective study included biopsy-confirmed female patients diagnosed with LABC meeting the selection criteria and attending surgical , medical and radiation oncology departments of a tertiary care centre in south India , from April 2013 to December 2014. Conventional workup included serum chemistry , mammogram , bone scan , contrast-enhanced CT ( CECT) chest and upper abdomen and ultrasound abdomen and pelvis.
