 A cross-sectional survey was conducted to determine which personal and environmental characteristics influenced diversity of participation in activities outside school for typically developing children and adolescents. Four hundred twenty-two ( 215 boys) typically developing Australian children ( mean = 11.0 yr) completed the Children 's Assessment of Participation and Enjoyment ( CAPE) and the Preferences for Activity of Children to describe their participation on five activity types: Recreational , Active Physical , Social , Self-Improvement , and Skill-Based.
