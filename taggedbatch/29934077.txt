 Mucormycosis is a relatively uncommon but highly aggressive fungal infection caused by the Mucormycotina subgroup of fungi<pathogen>. Mucormycosis usually affects the skin , sinuses , or lungs of an immunocompromised host. Other risk factors for development of mucormycosis include hematologic malignancy , intravenous drug abuse , chronic steroid use , and contaminated traumatic wounds<disease><symptom>. Cutaneous mucormycosis of the hand involving the skin and soft<disease> tissues is rare but has been reported previously. In this case report , we review the clinical course of a patient with mucormycosis of the hand who was found to have extensive soft<disease> tissue and bony destruction and ultimately required amputation.
