 Consort dermatitis<disease> refers to an allergic contact dermatitis<disease> caused by transfer from an intimate contact to a sensitized patient. Although close contact with other humans most commonly provokes consort dermatitis<disease> , pets have been the source in a minority of cases. We present a unique case of transfer dermatitis<disease> from a patient 's cat litter to her forearms. Pediatric dermatologists should be aware of the possibility of consort or `` transfer '' allergic contact dermatitis<disease> from pets.
