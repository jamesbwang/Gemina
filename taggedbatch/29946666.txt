 Morbid obesity has reached epidemic proportions in the United States and constitutes a significant cause of morbidity and mortality. Bariatric surgery represents a viable and effective means of weight<symptom> loss<symptom>. Laparoscopic adjustable gastric band placement is the most commonly used and least invasive bariatric surgical technique. Although the complication rate is low , various complications have been described , including erosion of the gastric band into the stomach. The authors present a case of laparoscopic adjustable gastric band erosion , where both the band and the tubing eroded into the stomach and colon , and the tubing further eroded out of and back into the colon several times.
