 Subcutaneous<disease> fat necrosis<symptom> of the newborn is a rare benign panniculitis found in term and post-term neonates. Diagnosis is based on clinical characteristics and specific alterations in the adipocytes , detected by anatomical pathology. Subcutaneous<disease> fat necrosis<symptom> of the newborn can occur in uncomplicated pregnancy and childbirth. However , perinatal complications such as asphyxia , hypothermia<symptom> , seizures<symptom> , preeclampsia , meconium aspiration , and even whole-body cooling used in newborns with perinatal hypoxia/anoxia may be associated with this entity.
