 Cancer of the urethra is very rare with an age-adjusted incidence of only 0.6 per million women in Europe. The etiology is multifactorial and the incidence increases with age , with the highest rates in patients 75 years or older. We herein describe a 58-year-old woman referred to our unit due to pollakisuria and repeated lower urinary<disease> tract<disease> infections<disease>. The gynecological examination revealed a suspect area in the anterior wall of vagina. Subsequently , ultrasound examination , MRI , and PET-CT scan followed by vaginal biopsies revealed a urethral adenocarcinoma.
