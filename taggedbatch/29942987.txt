 Nonsuicidal self-injury ( NSSI) is a serious public health concern among adolescents. Identifying risk factors of NSSI is important to effectively prevent or reduce such behavior. Child maltreatment is one of the most widely recognized risk factors for NSSI. How child maltreatment and NSSI is related , however , is still unclear. The present study tested the temporal relationship between physical and emotional abuse and NSSI , with distress intolerance as the potential mediator.
