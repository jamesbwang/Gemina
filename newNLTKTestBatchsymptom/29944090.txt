 Maintenance<GPE> of weight loss after behavioral intervention tends to be poor, and there is need for an improved understanding of factors that are associated with successful maintenance. Social<PERSON> comparison is known to be a powerful influence on treatment outcomes for group-based behavioral weight loss programs, but little is known about the role of individual differences in social comparison orientation ( i.e., tendency to value comparison information ) in this context. The goal of this study was to examine prospective relations between social comparison orientation and long-term weight loss outcomes ( percent weight loss, aerobic-intensity physical activity ) among participants in behavioral weight loss treatment.
