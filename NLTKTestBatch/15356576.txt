 Epidermodysplasia verruciformis ( EV) is an autosomal recessive skin disorder with a phenotype conditional on human beta-papillomavirus<pathogen> ( beta-HPV) infection<disease>. Such infections<disease> are common and asymptomatic in the general population , but in individuals with EV , they lead to the development of plane wart-like and red or brownish papules or pityriasis versicolor-like skin<symptom> lesions<symptom> , from childhood onwards. Most patients develop non-melanoma skin cancer ( NMSC) , mostly on areas of UV-exposed skin , from the twenties or thirties onwards.
