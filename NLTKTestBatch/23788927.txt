 Basaloid squamous cell carcinoma ( BSCC) is a rare and aggressive variant of oral squamous cell carcinoma with a predilection for the tongue and in other locations , such as floor of the mouth<symptom> , palate , retromolar trigone , and gingival mucosa. Here , we present a case of BSCC of oropharynx in a 60-year-old male patient.
