 Nonvariceal , nonulcer upper gastrointestinal hemorrhage<symptom> ( UGIH) is a less common cause for acute upper gastrointestinal bleeding. However , nonvariceal , nonulcer UGIH is an important entity to identify and treat appropriately to prevent bleeding-related morbidity and mortality. Over the past 40 years , there has been a revolution in gastrointestinal endoscopy and a similar revolution in the management of UGIH. The aim of this article is to focus on the endoscopic management of nonvariceal , nonulcer UGIH , with a focus on the newer diagnostic and treatment modalities currently available.
