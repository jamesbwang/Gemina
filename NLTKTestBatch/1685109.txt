 Multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 ( MEN1) syndrome<symptom> is characterized by combined occurrence of tumors of endocrine glands including the parathyroid , the pancreatic islet cells , and the anterior pituitary gland. Parathyroid involvement is the most common manifestation and usually the first clinical involvement in MEN1 syndrome<symptom> , followed by gastroentero-pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors ( NETs).
