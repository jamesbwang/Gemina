 In addition to being a passage for sperm , menstruum , and the baby , the human vagina and its microbiota can influence conception , pregnancy , the mode and timing of delivery , and the risk of acquiring sexually transmitted infections<disease>. The physiological status<symptom> of the vaginal milieu is important for the wellbeing of the host as well as for successful reproduction. High estrogen states , as seen during puberty and pregnancy , promote the preservation of a homeostatic ( eubiotic) vaginal microenvironment by stimulating the maturation and proliferation of vaginal epithelial cells and the accumulation of glycogen.
