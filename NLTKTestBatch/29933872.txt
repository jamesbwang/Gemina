 Extramedullary hematopoisis ( EMH) normally occurs in fetal life , but it is pathological in later life and most of the time because of underlying marrow diseases<disease>. Sometimes EMH tissue can present with large masses which can cause compressive and constitutional symptoms<symptom>. They can be wrongly diagnosed as malignancy and pulmonary tuberculosis<disease>. Here in this case report we are reporting a case with mediastinal EMH because of underlying mylofibrosis.
