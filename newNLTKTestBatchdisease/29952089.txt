 The onset of psoriasis is often seen in HIV infection, called HIV-associated psoriasis. Although HIV-associated psoriasis is usually refractory, there are some cases relieved only by antiretroviral therapy. In those cases, the pathogenesis may be formed differently from psoriasis vulgaris. We present the case of a 42-year-old Japanese<GPE> man with HIV-associated psoriasis. The patient developed a systemic scaly eruption, especially on the soles. Histopathological examination showed typical psoriatic findings and plasma cell infiltration into the dermis.
