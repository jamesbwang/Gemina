  Encephalitozoon<pathogen> cuniculi<pathogen> is an obligate intracellular microsporidian parasite that commonly induces subclinical infections in rabbits , but occurs also in a range of other species , including various rodents , carnivores , humans and birds. The present report describes encephalitozoonosis<disease> in a group of captive Barbary striped grass mice ( Lemniscomys barbarus) in a zoo collection. The aetiology was confirmed by immunohistochemistry and PCR with subsequent sequencing. The source of infection is not known.
