  As of 2017 , the international football federation introduced the change of direction ability test ( CODA) and the 5 × 30 m sprint test for assistant referees ( ARs) and continued the 6 × 40 m sprint test for field referees ( FRs) as mandatory tests. The aim of this study was to evaluate the association between performance in these tests and running performance during matches at the top level in Norway. The study included 9 FRs refereeing 21 matches and 19 ARs observed 53 times by a local positioning system at three stadiums during the 2016 season. Running performance during matches was assessed by high-intensity running ( HIR) distance , HIR counts , acceleration distance , and acceleration counts.
