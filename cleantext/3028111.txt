  Though hemorrhaging pelvic arteries can be treated with transcatheter arterial embolization , difficulty in selection of injured arteries can lead to increased morbidity and mortality due to embolization of untargeted vasculature and prolonged time to hemostasis , resulting in unnecessary ischemic injury and prolonged hemorrhage<symptom>. This report describes a balloon occlusion technique utilized in the setting of an unselectable hemorrhaging obturator artery.
