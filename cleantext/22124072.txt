  Oral myiasis is invasion of the soft tissues of oral cavity by the parasitic larvae of the flies. This condition affects debilitated , mentally challenged individuals. These patients are not physically or mentally endowed to ward off flies or complain about worms burrowing their way into oral wounds<disease> This study describes three cases of orofacial myiasis , removal of the live maggots , supportive treatment , and management of the cases with application of turpentine oil and also highlights oral health care in the patients with special needs.
