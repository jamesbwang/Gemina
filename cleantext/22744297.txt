  Given the limited research into the physiological and psychological demands of elite synchronised swimming , the aim of this study was to examine 10 elite female synchronised swimmers and analyse the relationship between training load , stress , illness episodes , and salivary biomarkers during a period of training and competition. Saliva samples were collected before ( BASE) , during an intensified training camp ( CAMP) , during an international competition period ( COMP) , and post competition recovery ( REC) for analysis of cortisol , testosterone , and secretory immunoglobulin A ( SIgA).
