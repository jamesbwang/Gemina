  Recognizing eating disorders in the pediatric population can be challenging for outpatient providers. With the high prevalence of these disorders in children and adolescents , it is critical that pediatricians recognize these disorders and connect these children and families with available treatments. This article provides a review of the current diagnostic criteria for pica , rumination disorder , anorexia<symptom> nervosa , bulimia nervosa , avoidant restrictive food intake disorder , binge-eating disorder , other specified feeding or eating disorder , and unspecified feeding or eating disorder as described in The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders , fifth edition. Recommendations for initial medical evaluation and helpful screening measures are discussed. ( Pediatr Ann. 2018; 47 ( 6): e244-e249.).
