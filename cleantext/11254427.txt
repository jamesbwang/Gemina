  Acute compartment syndrome<disease> in athletes is a rare orthopedic emergency associated with strenuous exercise. It is often diagnosed late and can lead to severe complications and high morbidity. This report describes the case of a young soccer player with acute compartment syndrome<disease> with no history of trauma , diagnosed and treated 24 h after the onset of symptoms , through minimally invasive decompressive fasciotomy , with good postoperative evolution.
