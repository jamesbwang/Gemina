  A pediatric mandibular fracture can cause a child severe<symptom> pain<symptom> and the parent or caregiver extreme worry. While the pattern of fractures in children is similar to adults , however , due to a number of factors , including the anatomical complexity of the developing mandible , management of such fractures differs from that of adults and can greatly challenge the pediatric dentist. Various treatment modalities of managing mandibular fracture are available , such as closed/open cap splint with circummandibular wiring , arch-bar fixation , and cementation of the cap splint.
