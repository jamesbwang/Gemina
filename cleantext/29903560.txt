  This article describes the current applications of various technologies based on either autofluorescence or near-infrared light illumination , tailored to aid practitioners in detecting and quantitatively monitoring oral diseases such as dental<disease> caries<disease> and oral cancer at the earliest stage of their formation , or in the conservative surgical excision of necrotic bones in diseases such as chronic osteomyelitis<disease> , osteoradionecrosis , and medication-related osteonecrosis of the jaw. The data discussed are primarily based on published scientific studies and reviews from case reports , clinical trials , and in vitro and in vivo studies. References have been traced manually , by MEDLINE , or through manufacturer 's websites.
