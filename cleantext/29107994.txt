  The evidence for the effectiveness of humor-based positive psychology interventions ( PPIs; i.e. , interventions aimed at enhancing happiness and lowering depressive symptoms) is steadily increasing. However , little is known about who benefits most from them. We aim at narrowing this gap by examining whether personality traits and sense of humor moderate the long-term effects of humor-based interventions on happiness and depressive symptoms.
