  May-Hegglin anomaly ( MHA) is an inherited autosomal dominant disorder characterized by giant platelets and inclusion bodies in granulocytes , and thrombocytopenia<symptom>. There is no consensus on the perioperative management of this disorder. We report a case involving a patient with MHA who was perioperatively managed without platelet transfusion for cervical laminectomy and laminoplasty.
