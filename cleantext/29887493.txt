  After tendon injuries , it has been observed clinically that patient-reported disability is more severe than the patient 's actual performance. This is thought to result from a fear of movement ( kinesiophobia) after surgery. The aim of this study was to investigate the presence of kinesiophobia in this patient population and its effects on the clinical outcomes. Patients ( n = 118) with tendon repairs were included.
