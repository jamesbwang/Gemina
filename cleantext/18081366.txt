  The aim of this study was to determine the sensitivity of selected subjective and objective monitoring assessments in detecting changes in group and individual responses to low and high load bouts of high intensity intermittent exercise. In a counterbalanced crossover design , Thirteen Academy Rugby Union players ( mean ± SD: age: 18 ± 1 years) performed a low load ( 15 min) and a high load ( 90 min) bout of high intensity intermittent exercise ( Loughborough Intermittent Shuttle Test) one week apart. Monitoring assessments were performed immediately prior to and 20 h following each trial.
