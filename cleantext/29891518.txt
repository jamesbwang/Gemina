  This case report presents a male patient with epithelioid mesothelioma that was initially misdiagnosed as cancer of unknown primary ( CUP) and correctly identified using molecular panel sequencing. The patient had a prior history of colon and breast cancer. To assess the enlarged mediastinal lymph nodes , retrosternal lymphadenectomy was performed in 2016. The lymph nodes were histologically deemed unrelated to the known breast cancer by the reference pathologist , thus leading to the diagnosis of a CUP syndrome<disease>. When the patient presented to our center , targeted deep sequencing of both breast cancer and presumed CUP was performed to address the clonal relationship between both malignancies.
