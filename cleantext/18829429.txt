  Many patients with substance use disorders ( SUDs) live in a stressful environment , and comorbidity is not uncommon. Understanding the neural mechanisms underlying heroin and nicotine dependences and their relationships to social cognition could facilitate behavioral therapy efficacy. We aimed to provide a translational approach that leads to identifying potential biomarkers for opioid use disorder ( OUD) susceptibility during recovery.
