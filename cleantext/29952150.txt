  Acute pancreatitis<disease> is a potentially life-threatening condition primarily associated with gallstones or prolonged and excessive alcohol intake. Although the initial triggers of the condition can vary , the resulting pathophysiology is broadly similar irrespective of the cause. This article explores the pathophysiology of the main causes of acute pancreatitis<disease> , and discusses nursing management of the condition in the acute setting and the long-term issues to consider. It also outlines the conservative management of the condition , which includes pain management , provision of fluids and nutritional care.
