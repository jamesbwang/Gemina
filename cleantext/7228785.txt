  Duodenitis-proximal jejunitis ( DPJ) is an inflammatory process of the proximal part of the small intestine and occurs sporadically in horses. It is clinically characterized by an acute onset of ileus and nasogastric reflux leading to systemic signs of toxemia<symptom>. This review discusses the definition of the disease , potential etiologic agents , clinical findings , epidemiological features , histopathologic and clinico-pathological findings , and medical management of this condition.
