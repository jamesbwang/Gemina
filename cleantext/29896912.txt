  Household air pollution from biomass cookstoves is estimated to be responsible for more than two and a half million premature deaths annually , primarily in low and middle-income countries where cardiometabolic disorders , such as Type II Diabetes , are increasing. Growing evidence supports a link between ambient air pollution and diabetes , but evidence for household air pollution is limited.
