  Cutaneous nerve hyperplasia<disease> is characterized by the presence of increased and hypertrophic myelinated and unmyelinated nerve fibers in the dermis. We report a case of periadnexal nerve hyperplasia<disease> as an asymptomatic and infrequent reactive histopathological change , with no associated risk of malignancy that can be seen in scars , in patients with no syndromic stigmata.
