  Transarterial chemoembolization ( TACE) combines intra-arterial delivery of a chemotherapeutic agent with selective embolization to obtain a synergistic effect. TACE is recognized as the standard treatment of hepatocellular carcinoma patients at an intermediate stage. If conventional TACE , defined as the injection of an emulsion of a drug with ethiodized oil , still has a role to play , the development of drug-eluting beads has allowed many improvements and optimization of the technique.
