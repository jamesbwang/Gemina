  Prolonged exposure to microbial products such as lipopolysaccharide can induce a form of innate immune memory that blunts subsequent responses to unrelated pathogens , known as lipopolysaccharide tolerance. Sepsis<disease> is a dysregulated systemic immune response to disseminated infection that has a high mortality rate. In some patients , sepsis<disease> results in a period of immunosuppression ( known as ` immunoparalysis ')
