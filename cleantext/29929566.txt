  A seven-month-old girl was referred to the emergency department ( ED) after a general practitioner suspected Steven-Johnson syndrome<disease>. Actually , the diagnosis of bullous pemphigoid ( BP) was made based on biopsies; BP is a rare , autoimmune skin disease involving the presence of blisters known as bullae. The child was efficiently treated with topical steroids. This case shows the importance of the ED physician 's prior knowledge of BP so that a differential diagnosis with other autoimmune diseases ( dermatosis , pemphigus) can be made.Thabouillot O , Le Coz J , Roche NC. Bullous pemphigoid in an infant: a case report. Prehosp Disaster Med.
