  In this editorial , we aimed to provide an outline of the world history of liver transplantation ( LT) , with a special focus on the innovation , development , and current controversies of living donor ( LD) LT from East Asian and Western perspectives. In 1963 , Starzl et al. ( University of Colorado , U.S.) performed the world 's first human LT for a 3-year-old child with biliary atresia ( 1).
