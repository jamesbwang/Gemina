  Poor<symptom> synchrony between the delivery of mechanical breaths , the neural respiratory timing , and needs of patients is relatively frequent under mechanical ventilation in the intensive care unit. This review summarizes the current knowledge on the different types of dyssynchrony described to date , their mechanism , consequences , and potential management. There is still a long way to get to a comprehensive knowledge and uncertainties remain. Ongoing research and development of monitoring tools are urgently needed to allow a better appraisal of this area in a near future.
