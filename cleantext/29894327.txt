  Fistulas<disease> are defined as abnormal connections between 2 organ , vessels , or structures. They can often present in the genitourinary tract as a result of iatrogenic injury during pelvic surgery. A 46-year-old female presents many years after a hysterectomy for persistent vaginal leakage with concern for a vesicovaginal fistula<disease>. Computed tomography urogram , intravenous retrograde pyelogram , and cystoscopy were negative for vesicovaginal fistula<disease>; however , a vaginal sinus tract was noted and further explored.
