  Prospective motor control moves the body into the future , from where one is to where one wants to be. It is a hallmark of intentionality. But its origin in development is uncertain. In this study , we tested whether or not the arm movements of newborn infants were prospectively controlled. We measured the spatiotemporal organization of 480 full-term neonatal arm movements and 384 arm movements of prematurely born infants at-risk for neurodevelopmental disorder.
