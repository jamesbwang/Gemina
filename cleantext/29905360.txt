  The microbiome is defined as the total of cellular microorganisms of baczerial , viral or e. g. , parasite origin living on the surface of a body. Within the anatomical areas of otorhinolaryngology , a significant divergence and variance can be demonstrated. For ear , nose , throat<symptom> , larynx and cutis different interactions of microbiome and common factors like age , diet and live style factors ( e. g. , smoking) have been detected in recent years. Besides , new insights hint at a passible pathognomic role of the microbiome towards diseases in the ENT area. This review article resumes the present findings of this rapidly devloping scientific area.
