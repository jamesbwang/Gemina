  Extranodal NK/T-cell lymphoma nasal type is a rare disease that mainly affects the nasal cavity and paranasal sinuses of males in the fifth decade of life. It has aggressive and locally destructive behaviour , and can be complicated by the hemophagocytic syndrome<disease> , conferring high lethality to the disease. This article describes a case of NK/T-cell lymphoma nasal type in a previously healthy patient , exemplifying its rapid and fulminant course.
