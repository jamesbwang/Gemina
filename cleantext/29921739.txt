  To study posttraumatic stress in patients after treatment in an intensive care unit ( ICU). This prospective cohort study included 136 adult patients with critical medical and surgical problems who were discharged from the ICU of the Caritas Medical Centre , Hong Kong. Their occurrence of posttraumatic stress disorder ( PTSD) , anxiety<symptom> , and depression<symptom> after ICU treatment were measured using the Impact of Event Scale-Revised and Hospital Anxiety<symptom> and Depression<symptom> Scale. Patient ICU experience was measured using the ICU Memory Tool. Multivariable analyses were conducted to examine the predictors of PTSD symptoms , anxiety<symptom> , and depression<symptom> Symptoms of PTSD , anxiety<symptom> , and depression<symptom> were reported in 10 % to 17 % of patients. Symptom severity was associated with less factual memory , more vivid memory of feelings about and more delusional memory of the ICU experience , low emotional support , and high perceived life threat. Symptoms of posttraumatic stress , anxiety<symptom> , and depression<symptom> may occur after ICU treatment. Early identification and appropriate intervention for PTSD are important for rehabilitation.
