  Any form of congenital nevus in a child is a cause of parental anxiety<symptom>. Parents are often worried about the future course of the lesion<symptom>. Some of these lesions<symptom> grow in extent with the child 's growth and involve other body areas; others grow proportionate to the involved body part , giving an impression of apparent growth of the lesion<symptom>. We use an illustrative `` blowing a balloon '' technique to explain the apparent growth pattern of some congenital nevi.
