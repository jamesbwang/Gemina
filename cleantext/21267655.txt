  People seeking asylum in high-income countries are vulnerable to food insecurity due to limited opportunities for social and economic participation. While charity organizations have long sought to provide food aid to those in need , the increasing number of people seeking this assistance requires alternatives. Using a case study approach , this research investigates The Food Justice Truck , which is a social enterprise designed to provide low cost , nutritious food to people seeking asylum with an aim to reduce the food insecurity burden.
