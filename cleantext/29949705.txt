  The wide range and vague nature of clinical presentations of thyroid emergencies make accurate and timely diagnosis challenging. Patients with a variety of thyroid conditions present to the emergency department , and appropriate suspicion can reduce unnecessary delay and expense in determining the correct diagnosis. This issue reviews the current evidence for presentation , evaluation , and treatment for emergencies of thyroid function and anatomy including hypothyroidism , hyperthyroidism , thyroid nodules<symptom> , and thyroid trauma. Complications of thyroid dysfunction are also considered , as well as recommendations for disposition and follow-up.
