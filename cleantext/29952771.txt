  Specific or nonspecific findings of sexual abuse may be observed upon examination of a child. Examination of the genital area is extremely important for the identification of child sexual abuse ( CSA). However , medical conditions detected in the child 's genital area may be misinterpreted as signs of CSA. Herein , we present a case of a child with suspected CSA who was treated in the intensive care unit. The following incidental findings were obtained upon anal examination: dilatation , ecchymosis , laceration , and hemorrhage<symptom>. This report aims to discuss the anal examination findings that can mimic CSA.
